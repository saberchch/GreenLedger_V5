Herding the Data Cats

The hardest part of carbon accounting isn't math. It's getting the right numbers out of different departments, correctly formatted, on time, with adequate proof.

02 The Data Maturity Ladder

You will never have perfect data in Year 1. The goal is to establish a baseline using Level 1 data, and systematically upgrade specific categories to Level 2 and Level 3 in following years.

Level 1

Spend Data

Extracted directly from the ERP or accounting software (e.g. Total $ spent on flights).

Easy to Collect
Level 2

Activity Data (Physical)

Physical units extracted from operational systems (e.g. Total kWh from utility bills, Tons of steel).

Medium Effort
Level 3

Primary Supplier Data

Exact emissions data provided by the supplier (e.g. Specific LCA for a Dell Laptop model).

High Effort

03 The A-B-C Workflow

A

Assign

Identify the single "Data Owner" for each category. Do not email "The HR Team", email "Sarah in HR."

B

Boundaries

Define exactly what data you need (e.g. "Electricity in kWh for the Paris office between Jan 1 and Dec 31").

C

Collect Evidence

Numbers without proof will fail an audit. Every data point must be backed by a verifiable document.

04 Proof is Paramount

Data entered into GreenLedger must be auditable. A third-party verifier must be able to trace every emission metric back to the original source document.

✗

Weak Evidence

Email: "Hey, I think we used about 50,000 kWh last year."
Slack message: "I asked Dave, he says it's basically the same as 2024."
Notepad screenshot: A blurry photo of handwritten numbers.
✓

Audit-Ready Evidence

description Original PDF Utility Bill (e.g. EDF Invoice)
