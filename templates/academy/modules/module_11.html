02 The Mitigation Hierarchy

You cannot simply buy your way out of emissions. Genuine decarbonization follows a strict order of operations known as the Mitigation Hierarchy.

1. Avoid

e.g., Replace business flights with virtual meetings.

2. Reduce

e.g., Optimize freight routes to use less fuel per delivery.

3. Replace

e.g., Switch from diesel trucks to electric vehicles.

LAST RESORT

4. Offset & Neutralize

For residual emissions only (buying carbon credits).

04 The Low-Hanging Fruit

Always start on the left side of the MACC Curve. These are "Negative Cost" projects—they have a positive ROI, meaning they reduce carbon AND increase company profits over time.

lightbulb

LED Retrofitting

Replacing old halogen bulbs with LEDs across all facilities. Payback period is usually under 2 years.

ROI: +35%
hvac

HVAC Optimization

Installing smart sensors to turn off heating/cooling in empty rooms. Drastically cuts Scope 2.

ROI: +20%
directions_car

Virtual Meetings

Enforcing a policy to substitute 30% of domestic business travel with Teams/Zoom calls. Zero capital cost.

ROI: +100%

05 Strategic Supplier Engagement

For most companies, 80%+ of emissions sit in Scope 3 (Purchased Goods). You cannot reduce these directly; you must leverage your purchasing power to pressure suppliers.

1

Insert carbon requirements into RFP (Request for Proposal) contracts.

2

Mandate that Tier 1 suppliers provide life-cycle assessments (LCAs) for their parts.

3

Shift procurement spend toward lower-carbon alternatives if current suppliers fail to adapt.
