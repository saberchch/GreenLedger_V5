Navigating the Factors

Without accurate Emission Factors (EFs), activity data is useless. Learn how to source, evaluate, and select the right multipliers for your carbon ledger.

02 Public vs. Commercial

While public databases cover 90% of a standard corporate footprint, complex manufacturing often requires commercial Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) databases.

lock_open Public Databases

  • ✓ Free to access and transparent rules.
  • ✓ Best for: Energy, Transport, Waste, basic materials (steel, concrete).
  • ✕ Weakness: Very limited for specific complex products (e.g., "iPhone 14 Pro Motherboard").
Examples: EPA (US), ADEME (FR)

lock Commercial Databases

  • ✓ Massive, granular academic datasets (10,000+ factors).
  • ✓ Best for: Specific chemicals, electronics, complex agriculture.
  • ✕ Weakness: Expensive licenses ($5k+), often "black box" methodologies.
Examples: Ecoinvent, Agri-Footprint

03 The Selection Hierarchy

If you search "Electricity" you will find 500 different factors. How do you choose? Auditors look for adherence to the following hierarchy.

1. Specificity (Technology)

A factor for "Apple Macbook Pro 14" is better than "Laptop Computer".

2. Geography

If no exact product match exists, match the region. "Server in Germany" is better than "Server Global Average".

3. Recency (Time)

If you must choose between two similar proxy factors, pick the one published most recently (technology becomes more efficient over time).

05 Data Quality Rating (DQR)

You can evaluate the reliability of your footprint using a DQR matrix (score 1-5, where 1 is perfect). It evaluates both the Activity Data and the Emission Factor.

Score Activity Data Quality Emission Factor Match
1 - Excellent Metered physical data (exact kWh read from a smart meter). Exact technology, exact geography, less than 2 years old.
3 - Fair Extrapolated data (estimated based on square footage). Similar technology, regional proxy, less than 5 years old.
5 - Poor Spend-based estimations ($). Global average factor, older than 10 years.
