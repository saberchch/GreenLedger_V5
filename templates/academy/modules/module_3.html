2 — Organizational Boundaries

Before you can count emissions, you must decide "who" you are. In complex corporations with many subsidiaries, joint ventures, and investments, this is the most critical step.

Equity Share

You account for emissions according to your share of equity in the operation. If you own 40% of a company, you report 40% of its emissions.

Best For

Lining up climate reporting with financial accounting ownership.

Control Approach

You account for 100% of emissions from operations over which you have control. Equity percentage doesn't matter here.

Financial

Directing policies for economic gain.

Operational

Having authority to implement rules.

3 — The Three Scopes in Depth

To help companies manage their climate impact and avoid double counting, the GHG Protocol categorizes emissions into three "Scopes." Let's break them down by their physical reality.

factory

Scope 1

Direct Emissions

Mandatory

These are emissions that happen directly from sources you own or control. They come from your chimneys and exhausts.

Fugitive

bolt

Scope 2

Energy Indirect

Mandatory

These are emissions from the generation of purchased energy (electricity, steam, heat, cooling) that your company consumes.

Location-based

Reflects the average emissions intensity of the local power grid where consumption occurs.

Market-based

Reflects emissions from the specific energy products or contracts you have chosen (e.g. REC certificates).

hub

Scope 3

Value Chain Indirect

Optional*

The "Elephant in the Room." This scope covers everything else in your value chain—from the raw materials you buy to how customers use your products. Often accounts for over 80% of a corporate footprint.

Upstream (Supply Side)

Purchased goods, Waste, Business travel, Employee commuting.

Downstream (Demand Side)

Use of sold products, End-of-life treatment, Logistics.

4 — Activity Data & Mapping Value Chain

Data is the lifeblood of carbon accounting. To calculate emissions, you need "Activity Data" — like liters of fuel or kilowatt-hours of electricity — and "Emission Factors."

The Data Collection Hierarchy

The more specific the data, the more accurate the report.

1

Direct Monitoring (Continuous)

Measuring gas flows at the exhaust. Rare, but the Gold Standard.

2

Activity-based (Fuel/Quantity)

Litters of petrol, Tons of waste, kWh. High accuracy.

3

Spend-based (EEIO Models)

Calculating emissions based on $ spent. Used for complex Scope 3 supply chain estimates.

5 — Calculating the Corporate Footprint

Now, we turn data into CO₂e. This is where physical activity meets climate science through the universal formula of carbon accounting.

The Master Formula

Activity Data × Emission Factor × GWP = Total CO₂e

Activity Data

Example: 15,000 kWh of office electricity used in 2023.

Emission Factor

Example: 0.25 kg CO₂e per kWh (from UK Gov or ADEME).

GWP

Global Warming Potential: Converting methane or N₂O into CO₂ equivalents.

7 — Key Principles (TACCR)

To ensure your report is "bankable" and audit-proof, you must follow the five core principles of the GHG Protocol.

Transparency

Disclose all assumptions and references.

Accuracy

Reduce uncertainties as much as possible.

Completeness

Don't leave out "inconvenient" sources.

Consistency

Use the same logic year over year.

Relevance

Focus on what matters for your industry.

The Principle Battle

Match the violation to the principle

"We decided not to report the emissions from our new factory because we couldn't find the exact gas meter reading yet."

Violation Completeness & Transparency

If data is missing, you must estimate it and disclose the gap.

"Last year we used UK Government factors, but this year we switched to a random online blog post factor because it was lower."

Violation Consistency & Accuracy

Methodological changes must be justified and previous years recalculated.

8 — Challenges & Common Pitfalls

Carbon accounting is a marathon of details. Even seasoned sustainability teams often fall into these three major traps.

warning

Double Counting

Reporting the same emission in Scope 1 and Scope 2. Remember: Scope 1 is ONLY the fuel YOU burn.

timer

Static Factors

Using 2018 emission factors for 2024 data. Grid intensities change every year as more renewables come online.

visibility_off

Boundary Drift

Forgetting to include a new subsidiary acquired mid-year. Your boundary MUST be consistent.
