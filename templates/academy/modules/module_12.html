01 Corporate vs. Product Level

Until now, we've measured the **GHG Corporate Standard**: the emissions of a company over one year. LCA uses the **Product Standard**: the emissions of a specific item over its entire biological life.

02 The Functional Unit

The most critical step in an LCA is defining the Functional Unit. You don't measure the item itself; you measure the service it provides. This ensures fair comparisons.

Which is more carbon efficient?

lightbulb

Incandescent Bulb

Manufacturing Footprint: 0.5 kg CO₂

Lifespan: 1,000 hours

VS
lightbulb

LED Bulb

Manufacturing Footprint: 1.5 kg CO₂

Lifespan: 25,000 hours

If we measured "1 Bulb" (the object), the LED looks worse (1.5kg vs 0.5kg).

But the Functional Unit should be "Providing 1,000 hours of light."
To get 25,000 hours of light, you need 25 Incandescent bulbs (12.5 kg CO₂) or just 1 LED bulb (1.5 kg CO₂). The Functional Unit reveals the true winner.

03 Cradle-to-Grave Timeline

An LCA tracks a product through 5 distinct life stages.

1. Raw Material Extraction

Mining, harvesting agriculture, pumping oil.

agriculture
factory

2. Manufacturing

Energy used to process materials into the final product.

3. Distribution

Freight from factory to warehouse to retail to customer.

local_shipping
power

4. Usage Phase

Energy/water consumed by the product while owned (e.g., washing a shirt, charging a phone).

5. End of Life

Landfill emissions, incineration, or recycling.

04 System Boundaries

Not all LCAs measure the full timeline. Companies must explicitly state their System Boundary so comparisons are fair.

Cradle-to-Gate

Measures only Extraction and Manufacturing. It stops the moment the product leaves the factory "gate".

12345

Cradle-to-Grave

The gold standard. Measures all 5 stages from raw extraction completely through to final disposal.
