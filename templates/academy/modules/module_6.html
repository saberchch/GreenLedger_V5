01 The Iceberg Effect

Scope 1 and 2 are highly visible (your fleets, your electricity bills). Scope 3 is everything else. It includes emissions across your entire supply chain, both upstream and downstream.

04 The Data Quality Maturation

Calculating Scope 3 is notoriously difficult because you don't own the data. Companies typically follow a maturation path from low-effort/low-accuracy to high-effort/high-accuracy.

1

Spend-Based Method LOW ACCURACY

Multiplying financial spend by environmentally-extended input-output (EEIO) factors.

$500,000 on Laptops × 0.4 kgCO2e/$ = 200 tCO₂e

Problem: If you buy premium $3000 laptops, your emissions look 3x higher than buying $1000 laptops, even if the carbon footprint is identical.

2

Average-Data Method MEDIUM ACCURACY

Replacing financial data with physical metrics (mass, volume, units) and using industry-average emission factors.

200 Laptops × 350 kgCO2e/unit = 70 tCO₂e

Problem: Still relies on industry averages, meaning you don't get credit if you choose a greener supplier.

3

Supplier-Specific Method GOLD STANDARD

Collecting primary data directly from the supplier based on their product life cycle assessments (LCAs).

Supplier LCA Data = 45 tCO₂e

Benefit: You actually capture the emissions reductions when you switch to low-carbon suppliers.
