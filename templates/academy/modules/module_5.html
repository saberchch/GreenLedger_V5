The Indirect Perimeter

Master the complexities of purchased energy. Understand the dual-reporting requirement, energy attribute certificates (EACs), and how to avoid greenwashing.

01 The Indirect Perimeter

Scope 2 accounts for GHG emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, steam, heating, and cooling consumed by your company. It is "indirect" because the actual emissions occur at the facility where the energy is generated, not where it is used.

bolt

Electricity

Power purchased from the grid for lighting, machinery, servers, and HVAC operations.

heat_pump

Thermal Energy

Purchased steam, heat, or cooling sourced from district heating/cooling systems or co-generation plants.

02 The Dual Reporting Requirement

The GHG Protocol requires companies operating in markets with contractual instruments (like the US, Europe, and Australia) to report Scope 2 emissions using two distinct methods.

The Physical Reality

Location-Based

Reflects the average emissions intensity of grids on which energy consumption occurs.

  • ✓ Uses regional/national grid average factors
  • ✓ Excludes company-specific green energy contracts
  • ✓ Shows what your physical facilities are actually drawing
The Financial Reality

Market-Based verified

Reflects emissions from electricity that companies have purposefully chosen regarding its origin.

  • ✓ Uses supplier-specific factors or certificates (RECs)
  • ✓ Allows companies to claim zero-emissions power
  • ✓ Drives market demand for renewable energy

04 Instruments of the Market

To claim zero-emission electricity under the market-based method, companies must purchase and retire Energy Attribute Certificates (EACs).

RECs

North America

Renewable Energy Certificates. Represents 1 MWh of renewable energy generated and delivered to the grid.

GOs

Europe

Guarantees of Origin. The standard tracking instrument required by the EU Renewable Energy Directive.

I-RECs

Rest of World

International RECs. Used in regions without national tracking systems (Asia, LatAm, Africa).

05 The Protocol Quality Criteria

You can't just buy any certificate from anywhere. The GHG Protocol mandates strict Scope 2 Quality Criteria to prevent double-counting and ensure legitimacy.

public

1. Market Boundaries

Certificates must be sourced from the same geographic market as the consuming facility. You cannot buy cheap Texas solar RECs to cover emissions for an office in London.

event

2. Vintage Alignment

Certificates must be produced as close as possible to the period of energy consumption, reasonably within the same calendar year.

block

3. Unique Claim & Retirement

Certificates must be tracked and permanently "retired" in a public registry on behalf of the reporting company to ensure no one else can claim that green megawatt.
