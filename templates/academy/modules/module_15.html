Green Finance & ESG

Capital markets are shifting. Understand how Green Bonds, Sustainability-Linked Loans, and ESG ratings are penalizing dirty companies and rewarding net-zero pioneers.

01 The Capital Shift

Historically, banks only looked at financial risk. Today, Climate Risk is Financial Risk. A factory ignoring sea-level rise or future carbon taxes is a bad loan candidate.

02 Financing Instruments

Organizations use two primary mechanisms to raise capital for sustainability initiatives: Green Bonds and Sustainability-Linked Loans (SLLs).

receipt_long
Use of Proceeds

Green Bonds

Capital must be spent on specific green projects.

expand_more

A company issues a bond to raise $100M. The legal contract dictates that 100% of this money must be spent to build a new wind farm. It cannot be used for general corporate purposes (like paying executive bonuses).

Risk: "Greenwashing" if the funds are diverted. Requires strict third-party auditing.
trending_down
General Purpose

Sustainability-Linked Loans (SLL)

Interest rates change based on corporate ESG performance.

expand_more

A company takes a $50M loan for general business use (payroll, marketing, etc.). However, the interest rate is tied to Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). If they reduce their Scope 1+2 emissions by 10% this year, their interest rate drops from 5% to 4.5%. If they fail, it rises to 5.5%.

Benefit: Incentivizes company-wide decarbonization without restricting how the capital is spent.
