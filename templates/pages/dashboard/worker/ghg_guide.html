Protocol Foundations

Accounting principles and organizational boundaries.

account_tree Defining Boundaries

Before calculating, we must decide what "counts." GreenLedger uses the Operational Control approach. If your company makes the decisions, the emissions belong in your report.

"If you pay the utility bill and manage the facility, it's under your operational control."

Knowledge Check

Your company leaks refrigerant from an AC unit in a building you rent and manage. Is this Scope 1?

visibility Reveal Answer
Yes! Since you have operational control over the facility, direct fugitive emissions (leaks) are categorized as Scope 1.
02

Mastering the Scopes

Deep dive into emission categorization.

factory

Scope 1 (Direct)

Emissions from sources that the company owns or controls directly.

Example

Burning natural gas in an on-site boiler.

bolt

Scope 2 (Energy)

Emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, heat, or steam.

Example

Electricity consumed by your office computers.

hub

Scope 3 (Value Chain)

All indirect emissions (not included in scope 2) that occur in the company's value chain.

Example

Emissions from a flight for a business trip.

Data Quality & Verification

Ensuring audit-ready records.

The Golden Rule: Evidence

A carbon entry without evidence is just a guess. To be audit-ready, every submission in GreenLedger should be backed by a document (Utility bill, Fuel receipt, Travel itinerary).

verified_user

Blockchain Notarization

Once your data is validated by your admin, GreenLedger generates a cryptographic proof. This "fingerprint" is stored on the ledger, making it impossible to change your data later without being detected. This is what makes your reporting trustworthy.

Knowledge Check

Why is evidence encrypted before being stored?

help_center VIEW EXPLANATION

To protect sensitive business information. Only authorized auditors and your org admins can decrypt and view the original evidence files.
